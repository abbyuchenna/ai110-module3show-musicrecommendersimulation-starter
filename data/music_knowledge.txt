[genre: pop]
pop music features catchy melodies, upbeat rhythms, and broad mainstream appeal. energy: medium to high (0.5–0.85). typical moods: happy, energetic. great for social settings, dancing, or feeling good. artists like taylor swift, dua lipa, and bruno mars define this sound.

[genre: lofi]
lofi hip hop is defined by mellow, lo-fidelity beats, soft vinyl crackle, and a calm, nostalgic atmosphere. energy: low (0.2–0.45). typical moods: chill, focused, relaxed. the go-to genre for studying, late-night work sessions, and unwinding. no lyrics, just vibes.

[genre: jazz]
jazz blends improvisation, complex harmonies, and expressive instrumentation — piano, saxophone, upright bass. energy: low to medium (0.2–0.55). typical moods: relaxed, moody, focused. works well as background music for coffee shops, late nights, or creative work.

[genre: rock]
rock music is guitar-driven with strong drums and an energetic, rebellious feel. energy: high (0.7–0.95). typical moods: intense, energetic, aggressive. perfect for working out, driving fast, or releasing stress. subgenres range from classic rock to metal.

[genre: electronic]
electronic music is produced entirely with synthesizers and drum machines. energy: medium to very high (0.5–1.0). typical moods: energetic, happy, focused, or intense depending on subgenre. works for dancing, raving, or focused deep work (ambient electronic).

[genre: metal]
metal is characterized by heavily distorted guitars, fast drumming, and aggressive or powerful vocals. energy: very high (0.85–1.0). typical moods: aggressive, intense. best for intense workouts, anger release, or listeners who want maximum adrenaline.

[genre: indie]
indie (independent) music has a raw, authentic, emotionally honest sound. energy: low to medium (0.3–0.65). typical moods: moody, relaxed, happy. associated with introspective moments, rainy days, road trips, and creative inspiration.

[genre: acoustic]
acoustic music uses unplugged, natural instrumentation — guitar, piano, voice. energy: low to medium (0.2–0.55). typical moods: relaxed, moody, happy. feels intimate and emotional. perfect for quiet mornings, sad days, or reflective evenings.

[genre: synthwave]
synthwave recreates 1980s electronic sounds — pulsing synths, driving beats, and retro atmosphere. energy: medium to high (0.55–0.85). typical moods: energetic, focused, moody. popular for late-night drives, coding sessions, and nostalgic vibes.

[genre: classical]
classical music uses orchestral instruments in structured compositions. energy: low to medium (0.1–0.5). typical moods: relaxed, focused. scientifically linked to improved concentration. ideal for studying, reading, or winding down before sleep.

[genre: ambient]
ambient music is atmospheric, texture-based, and minimalist — designed to create a space rather than demand attention. energy: very low (0.05–0.3). typical moods: relaxed, focused. used for meditation, sleep, background focus, and stress relief.

[genre: reggae]
reggae has a laid-back, off-beat rhythm rooted in jamaican music. energy: low to medium (0.3–0.6). typical moods: happy, relaxed, chill. associated with beach days, sunny afternoons, and a carefree mindset.

[genre: hip-hop]
hip-hop combines rhythmic rap vocals with sampled beats. energy: medium to high (0.5–0.85). typical moods: energetic, happy, moody. versatile — can be introspective or hype depending on the track.

[genre: country]
country music tells stories through guitar, fiddle, and heartfelt vocals. energy: low to medium (0.3–0.65). typical moods: happy, relaxed, moody. great for road trips, campfires, or nostalgic moments.

[mood: focused]

[mood: moody]

[mood: energetic]

[mood: aggressive]
